Study Title: Bias Assessment in AI-Powered Skin Lesion Detection Systems

IRB Approval: BUIRB-2025-001

Study Information

Purpose: This research aims to evaluate the fairness and effectiveness of artificial intelligence systems used for skin lesion detection, with particular focus on potential biases across different demographic groups.

Procedures

If you agree to participate, you will:

  • Provide demographic information (age, gender, ethnicity, skin type)
  • Upload or analyze skin lesion images using our AI system
  • Complete questionnaires about your experience
  • Provide feedback on AI predictions and interface usability

Duration

The study will take approximately 15-20 minutes to complete.

Risks and Benefits

Risks: There are minimal risks associated with this study. Some questions may cause mild discomfort when discussing personal health information.

Benefits: You will gain insight into AI-powered medical diagnosis systems and contribute to research that may improve healthcare equity.

Confidentiality

Your responses will be kept strictly confidential. All data will be anonymized and stored securely. No identifying information will be included in any publications or presentations.

Voluntary Participation

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw from the study at any time without penalty. You may skip any questions you prefer not to answer.

Contact Information

If you have questions about this research, please contact:

  • Research Team: medical-ai-research@bolton.ac.uk
  • Ethics Committee: ethics@bolton.ac.uk
  • Phone: +44 1204 900600

Data Storage and Usage

Data will be stored for 5 years as required by research guidelines. Anonymous results may be used in academic publications and presentations.
